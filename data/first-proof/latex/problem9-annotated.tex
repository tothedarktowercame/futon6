\section{Problem 9: Polynomial Detection of Rank-1 Scaling for Quadrifocal Tensors}

\subsection*{Problem statement}
Given generic camera matrices and quadrifocal tensors $Q^{(\alpha\beta\gamma\delta)}$, construct a polynomial map $F$ of degree bounded independently of $n$ that vanishes exactly on rank-1 separable scaling tensors $\lambda_{\alpha\beta\gamma\delta}=u_\alpha v_\beta w_\gamma x_\delta$.

\subsection*{Answer}
Yes: one can use degree-3 determinant constraints (all relevant $3\times 3$ minors).

\subsection*{Annotated proof sketch}
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=1.5em]
\item For fixed $(\gamma,\delta,k,\ell)$, the determinant pairing defines an alternating bilinear form of rank $2$ on $\mathbb R^4$, so all induced $3\times 3$ minors vanish.
\item If $\lambda$ is rank-1 separable, Hadamard-diagonal scaling preserves the rank-$\le 2$ property, hence all these minors vanish.
\item Conversely, vanishing of all minors across the three matricization patterns forces each matricization rank to be $1$, and compatibility implies full tensor separability.
\item Collect all such minors into $F$; each coordinate has degree $3$ and no dependence on camera parameters.
\end{enumerate}
