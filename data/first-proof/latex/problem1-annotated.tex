\section{Problem 1: Measure Equivalence Under Smooth Shifts of the $\Phi^4_3$ Measure}

\subsection*{Problem statement}
Given the renormalized $\Phi^4_3$ measure $\mu$ on $\mathbb{T}^3$, and a smooth nonzero shift $\psi$, determine whether $\mu$ and the shifted measure $T_\psi^*\mu$ are equivalent.

\subsection*{Answer}
Yes: $\mu \sim T_\psi^*\mu$.

\subsection*{Annotated proof sketch}
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=1.5em]
\item Use absolute continuity of $\mu$ with respect to the Gaussian reference measure $\mu_0$ from variational construction results for $\Phi^4_3$\footnote{Barashkov--Gubinelli, arXiv: \url{https://arxiv.org/abs/2003.10529}.}.
\item Apply Cameron--Martin to the Gaussian part: smooth shifts lie in the relevant Cameron--Martin space and preserve equivalence\footnote{Background discussion: \url{https://mathoverflow.net/questions/194307/cameron-martin-theorem-non-gaussian-shift}.}.
\item Control the interaction change $V(\phi-\psi)-V(\phi)$ using Wick expansion and exponential integrability for the cubic perturbation. This is the renormalized step tied to regularity structures / paracontrolled technology\footnote{Hairer, arXiv: \url{https://arxiv.org/abs/1303.5113}; Gubinelli--Imkeller--Perkowski, arXiv: \url{https://arxiv.org/abs/1210.2684}.}.
\end{enumerate}

The Radon--Nikodym derivative is strictly positive and integrable in both directions, so mutual absolute continuity follows.

\subsection*{Background and prerequisites}
\textbf{Field:} Stochastic PDE / Measure Theory.\\
\textbf{What you need:} Measure theory and Gaussian measures (Cameron--Martin theorem), plus familiarity with either regularity structures or paracontrolled distributions for making sense of the renormalized $\Phi^4_3$ interaction.\\
\textbf{Way in:} Hairer, ``An Introduction to Stochastic PDEs'' (lecture notes, arXiv:0907.4178) for the probabilistic side; the Barashkov--Gubinelli variational paper cited below is largely self-contained for the $\Phi^4_3$-specific machinery.
